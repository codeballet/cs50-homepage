Introduction

Johan Stjernholm is an acclaimed and award winning Choreographer, Artistic Director, and Performer. Based in London and working internationally, Johan is the founder and Artistic Director for the Space Engineering Art Project. In Beijing, China, Johan has also taken part in founding and co-directing the dance company Ballet Octahedron / Wu Tian Yuan, together with HRH Flora Zeta Cheongj-Leen.

As Artistic Director for Space Engineering Art Project has been producing ground-breaking art in venues across the world, such as the Victoria and Albert Museum and the Southbank in London, the Sherman Theatre in Cardiff, Wales, the Palazzo Theatre in Las Vegas, as well as in Canada, New York, South Korea, and China.

Working in South Korea, Johan has been producing and composing Opera Ballets. In 2016, he produced, directed, and choreograpehd The Golden Apple, and in 2017, he for the first time also composed the music for his second Opera: Desire Works (2017). Both these operas received critical appraise in their explorations of links between contemporary life and ancient, Norse mythology.

In China, Johan has been spearheading the development of contemporary performance and installation art, having created work in several of the most important museums and art galleries, including the Today Art Museum, the Three Shadows Museum, and the Xin Dong Cheng Space for Contemporary Art in the 798 District, Beijing.

In 2020, Johan's choreographic and performance art work in China was published as a chapter in The Routledge Companion to Dance Studies , edited by Helen Thomas and Stacey Prickett. His chapter, "The Scenography of Choreographing the Museum", explores a novel approach to the scenographic elements of performance, enabling generations of new dancers and performance artists to continue to advance contemporary embodied installation art.
